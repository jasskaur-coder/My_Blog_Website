Day 30 – “If Traffic Lights Worked Like C++ Programs”

← Back

August 11, 2025

What if…?

I was waiting at a traffic light today and suddenly thought: what if this thing ran on C++? Spoiler: chaos, but very fast chaos.

#include<traffic.h>

First hurdle - the light won’t even start unless someone remembered to include the right header file. Forget it, and the whole city freezes.
That's how life also works, right?... If we don't take right decision at the right time... The life won't give us opportunities....

Compilation Time

Green wants to turn on? Too bad, it has to compile first. Meanwhile, everyone’s stuck: me, two bikers, and an uncle on a scooty just staring at each other...
So some times we need patience in our life to compile the right thing....

Segmentation Fault

The light just dies mid-cycle. One car is halfway across, the rest are frozen. That’s undefined behavior on the streets.
Similarly life sometimes throw errors.. we just need to handle it...

Infinite Yellow Loop

Ever seen a signal stuck on yellow forever? That’s while(true) in action.
Need to correct it as soon as possible or else it will cause traffic jam...and will destroy lives...

Pointers Gone Wrong

Instead of changing this light, the program updates the one two streets away. Now my road is red, but somewhere else there’s complete anarchy.
So sometimes in life, we might focus our energy on the wrong place... while the real issue stays unsolved.

Final Thought

If traffic lights worked like C++ programs, we’d all be late. But hey, when it actually ran, it would be the fastest green light you’ve ever seen.
So yeah..! sometimes we can be stuck in the problems... But when we are finally out of it... we will be the fastest one...
